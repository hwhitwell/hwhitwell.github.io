About the LPDG

We are a free, local proteomics seminar series in the South East,
with a focus towards networking, discussion and supporting early career researchers.

The LPDG...

was founded to bring together the large community of proteomics scientists all working in and around London. We aim to provide a space for discussion, with a focus on methods and early career researchers (two fundamental building blocks of good research!), on all topics related to proteomics. The meetings comprise of research talks framed by a proteomics methods challenge, lunch, refreshments and pizza - they are free to attend thanks to sponsorship.

Webinar Programme


SARS-CoV-2: What Role Can Proteomics Play?

for 29th May 2020 14:00 BST
Last updated 15th May 2020

Prof Ray Iles

Direct Detection of Viral Envelope Proteins by MALDI-ToF MS, Sample Screening for COVID-19 and Beyond

The MALDI-ToF mass spectrometry is a clinical diagnostic platform utilising a highly-advanced method of protein pattern recognition to diagnose various diseases and health disorders. It uses dedicated digital data, analysed by AI algorithms and requires only a very small sample (either blood or urine or sputum) to deliver test results within minutes and at an economical cost level. The analysis is data rich and the sample can be re-interrogated with full scanning of all test panels from a single sample.

“I’ve spent the past ten years developing the use of MALDI-ToF mass spectrometry to detect a range of diseases by identifying their unique protein signature. Our technology delivers results within minutes and with fine tuning we aim to achieve this on COVID-19 but with far fewer false positives (greater specificity). Once developed this technology can provide a powerful and rapid first line in the control of any future new virus epidemics.” (Prof Ray Iles)

Dr Maarten Dhaenens

CoV-MS: A Universally Applicable Corona MRM Assay Developed as a Community Effort

Any exit strategy from the COVID-19 lockdown will require widespread testing. However, COVID-19 diagnosis is currently performed almost exclusively by qPCR. This has led to a worldwide constraint on testing and a global shortage of necessary reagents. We therefore pursued a complementary test, which detects SARS-CoV-2 proteins using mass spectrometry (MS). Within one month, we have developed this approach from discovery on high-resolution Q-TOF instruments, to a sensitive, 8-minute targeted MS assay of 17 marker peptides from NCAP_SARS2 and SPIKE_SARS2 proteins. Our protocol takes no more than five hours after sample arrival, with only ten minutes of hands-on work, and was designed to minimize sample use, time investment, reagent use and cost, and to facilitate parallelization. Moreover, we have shown on blind-coded clinical samples that our approach is feasible and highly promising. To streamline the transition from discovery to a clinically applicable assay, we have gathered a consortium to further optimize the Cov-MS test across 10 different labs on instruments from the top-three vendors world-wide. With an estimated testing capacity of 150 patients per day per instrument, our assay could achieve 10,000 additional tests per day in Belgium alone.

Dr Maarten Dhaenens
Dr Maarten Dhaenens Lab of Pharmaceutical Biotechnology,
Ghent University

As a biologist, I started working in MS and proteomics in 2005 during my masters in Med Biotech. Since, have worked in the lab for Pharmaceutical Biotechnology, focusing on proteomics in a plethora of different subjects, including rheumatology, conservation science, forensic proteomics and paleoproteomics. In 2008, we discovered a histone clipping event in Leukaemia cells and our research shifted almost exclusively to mining the histone code. We established DIA-MS by developing increasingly advanced peptide-centric data mining strategies (using predicted libraries) and a novel, more comprehensive data format - ion-networks. When corona struck, we immediately applied our expertise in DIA to developing a diagnostic assay that targets corona peptide biomarkers.

FAQ

Here is a list of answers to frequently asked questions for speakers, delegates and sponsors

  • 01. General FAQ
  • 02. For sponsors
  • 03. Webinars
How much is the conference to attend?

The meetings are free to attend! We raise money through the generous support of sponsors for refreshments and venue hire. Sponsors are offered the opporunity to suggest a plenary research presentaiton or present a short (10min) sales talk.

Are refreshments provided?

Yes! Thanks again to the support of our sponsors, we are able to provide a sandwhich buffet lunch, refreshments, and pizza and drinks to end. We try our best to get a range of refreshments to support diverse dietry requirments, but please let us know in advance if you have any specific needs.

How do we choose speakers?

We are always looking for new speakers - from PhD candidates to senior scientists within proteomics. We recruit speakers through personal invitations/networking, suggestions from deligates and from people contacting us directly. If you would like to present at a meeting, we would be delighted to hear from you, please get in touch at speaker@londonproteomics.co.uk

Do I need to register?

Registration is not essential, but it is very helpful for us to arrange catering and venues to ensure we don't over/under spend. In some cases, where space is limiting, registration may be essential and we will advertise this. If in doubt - register!

Is there any funding for travel?

We do not have any funding to support delegates. However, if you are speaking, it may be possible to get a travel grant from the British Society for Proteome Research (BSPR). In some instances, we may be able to subsidise travel costs for speakers, but this is dependant on sponsorship for that meeting.

Can I be involved in the organising committee?

The organising committee is made up of early career (including PhD candidates) and more experienced scientist from academia and industry. We are very keen to have a bredth of backgrounds and experience on the committee, so if you would like to be involved, please get in touch by speaking to a committee member at a meeting, or emailing us - we would like to hear from you!

Why sponser a meeting?

Our meetings are focused on proteomics and the delegates include proteomics-users from academia and industry. Therefore, at these meetings, there is a "captured audience" of possible clients. Sponsoring our meeting will allow you to present your product or service to a concentrated group of possible users. These meetings are only possible with sponsorship, and you will be contributing to the disemiation of science, and potentially meeting tallented future employees through networking.

How can I sponsor a meeting?

We have a range of options for meeting sponsorship. To sponsor a meeting, please get in touch at sponsor@londonproteomics.co.uk, we will be delighted to hear from you.

How much does it cost to sponsor a meeting?

  • Packages are flexible - if you are interested in sponsoring us but want something different, please get in touch at sponsor@londonproteomics.co.uk
  • Primary sponsor (e.g. big company): Option 1) plenary talk £1,500; Option 2) 5 minute sales presentation £1,250; Option 3) 10 minute sales presentation £2,000
  • Secondary sponsor (e.g. small company): Option 1) 5 minute sales presentation £750; Option 2) 10 minute sales presentation £1,250
  • Tertiary sponsor (e.g. start-up): Option 1) 5 minute sales presentation £500; Option 2) 10 minute sales presentation £800

Are webinars free to join?

Yes! Just like our physical meetings, our webinars are completely free to attend.

How do I register/attend the webinar?

Please register for our meeting in the normal way (click here). A link will be emailed to you prior to the meeting starting with instructions on how to join. Alternatively, a join link for the webinar will also be posted here immediately prior to it starting. For webinars run as a Microsoft Live Event, find instructions on how to join here, or using Zoom here.

How will discussion be mediated?

The discussion will be mediated by the chairperson who will take questions from all attendies. The best way to contribute during the talk and to post questions is through our slack account.

Are the webinars available online after the event?

The meetings are recored and will be posted to our archive page.

How are webinars funded?

We do not currently recieve any funding for our webinars. We are able to put them on with the generous support of other institution who allow us to use their webinar-enabled online communications accounts (e.g. Zoom).

If you still have unanswered questions after reading this page, wish to present a talk, suggest a venue or sponsor a meeting, please contact us.
